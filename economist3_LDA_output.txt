Iran and six world powers, led by America, struck the six-month interim nuclear agreement in the early hours of November 24th (see article). Saudi Arabia and Israel are at once troubled by the prospect of a redeemed Iran and also convinced that the whole idea is a dangerous illusion. This newspaper sees it differentlyin terms of both the risks and the rewards. Yet the real fantasy is to imagine that more sanctions or harsher negotiations could have produced a deal that was much better than this one. All this would take timeafter 34 years America and Iran have a lot of catching up to do. The supreme leader, though a sworn enemy of America, has given Mr Rohani licence to talk to the West.
